LA Kings
K-Town Night

The first NHL program built around Korean-American culture. As the only Korean employee at the LA Kings, I pitched the idea using ticketing and audience data, making the case for a real growth opportunity in LA's Korean community, and for celebrating Koreatown specifically rather than a generic "Korea Night." The seniors who played the anthem in hanbok went viral, reaching ESPN, the LA Times, CBS, and Sportsnet. It became the franchise's biggest viral moment since its Stanley Cup season.

A product builder who speaks data

Born in Seoul, I studied communications at the University of Iowa, then worked in marketing at the U.S. arm of game maker Com2uS. A realization sent me back to school: the decisions that mattered were all made with data, and I wanted to be the one making them. I earned an M.S. in Business Analytics at UC Irvine and joined the LA Kings as a business-intelligence analyst.

Today I work across the modern data stack, Fivetran into Databricks, a bronze / silver / gold warehouse, SQL transformations, and LLMs woven into the workflow, turning ticketing, web, and marketing data into decisions executives can act on. Outside the arena, I build: products, communities, and the occasional night that ends with eighty-year-olds playing harmonica on NHL ice.
