01The problem

Paper menus are expensive in ways restaurants stopped noticing. Every price change is a reprint. Every new dish needs a photoshoot. Laminated cards wear out, specials get taped on, and the menu, the single most important sales document in the room, is almost always out of date.

02What Listro does

Listro puts the menu on the guest's phone in one motion. The restaurant places an NFC card on the table or prints a QR; the guest taps or scans and instantly browses a fast, image-rich, mobile-first menu, no app to download. Tap, browse, order.

  • NFC cards and QR codes that open the menu in under a second
  • Customizable branding so the menu looks like the restaurant, not a template
  • Support for professional food photography without the per-dish photoshoot
  • Tap-to-order flow built directly into the menu

03Where it lives

Listro is live and running live demos at established rooms across cuisines, including Father's Office, Yabu, and The Lobster, starting at $25 a month. The pitch is simple enough to fit on the card: no photoshoots, no reprints, just a menu that's always current.
